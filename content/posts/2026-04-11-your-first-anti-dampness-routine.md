When internal dampness accumulates, it manifests on your skin as puffiness, enlarged pores, uneven tone, and that stubborn "greasy but dehydrated" sensation. The good news? ## Why This Routine Works

In TCM, dampness is excess moisture that the Spleen (your digestive and metabolic center) can't properly transform and transport. On skin, this translates to:

- Blocked pores from sluggish circulation
- Inflammation from trapped heat and toxins  
- Accelerated aging from oxidative stress in stagnant tissues

Your goal: move dampness OUT while keeping good moisture IN — what dermatologists call "balancing oil control with barrier repair."

The dampness connection: Harsh cleansers strip natural oils, triggering your skin to overproduce sebum — creating *more* dampness. Choose a low-pH gel or milky cleanser that respects your skin barrier.

What to look for:
- [Low-pH gel cleanser] — pH 5.0-6.0 maintains healthy moisture barrier
- [Centella Asiatica cleanser] — calms inflammation from damp-heat
